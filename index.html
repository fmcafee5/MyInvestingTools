Books

Common Stocks and Uncommon Profits

Written by Philip Fisher, this book offers insights into how to identify companies with significant growth potential. Fisher emphasizes qualitative analysis and long-term investment strategies.

Fortune's Formula

Written by William Poundstone, this book explains how Claude Shannon, John L. Kelly Jr., and others used the Kelly criterion to beat the market and the casino.

Margin of Safety

Written by Seth Klarman, this book outlines Klarman's investment philosophy and strategies for managing risk. He emphasizes the importance of having a margin of safety when making investment decisions.

One Up on Wall Street

Written by Peter Lynch, this book shares Lynch's investment philosophy and strategies that helped him achieve outstanding returns. He emphasizes the importance of researching and understanding the companies in which you invest.

Security Analysis: 6th Edition

Written by Benjamin Graham and David Dodd, this classic text lays the foundation for value investing. It provides a comprehensive guide to analyzing financial statements and assessing the intrinsic value of securities.

The Little Book of Common Sense Investing

Written by John Bogle, this book advocates for low-cost index fund investing. Bogle explains why most investors are better off investing in a diversified portfolio of low-cost index funds.

The Little Book That Beats the Market

Written by Joel Greenblatt, this book offers a simple formula for investment success, using a combination of high earnings yield and high return on capital.

Use the Magic Formula

The Intelligent Investor

Written by Benjamin Graham, this book is considered the bible of value investing. Graham's philosophy of "value investing" — protecting oneself from substantial error and teaching oneself to develop long-term strategies — has made The Intelligent Investor the stock market bible ever since its original publication in 1949.

The Big Secret for the Small Investor

Written by Joel Greenblatt, this book emphasizes the importance of low-cost investing and the benefits of index funds for small investors.
